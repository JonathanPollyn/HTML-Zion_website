DTA

Zion shall develop services that provide specialized sensory-motor, cognitive, communicative, social interaction and behavioral training to promote skill development for some portion of a 24-hour day (typically 8-9 hours) during work hours in the year. The program will provide weekday options for adult with developmental disabilities of all ages; teach members/consumer based on the ISP outcomes and provide the Support Coordinator the teaching strategies for the outcomes within 10 days of initiating service. DTA program is considering to be habilitation and provides for the personal care needs of the member.

The Objective and goal are to enhance and foster self-esteem, increase social and community safety skills, initiate development of new daily living skills, decrease or eliminate behaviors that are most challenging and/or disruptive to positive relationships. Zion Compassion Care, LLC (Zion) shall actively involve the consumer in developing monthly activity schedule. A copy of such schedule will be given to the client and the family, displayed in the facility and filed up. Zion shall maintain accurate record of the number of hours that each member/client spends daily in the facility by keeping a log of entry and exit at each point daily and monthly progress reports will be sent to the Support Coordinators.

The program will obtain information and carry out a pre-service orientation within the facility intimating them on how the program intends to meet the ISP outcomes. Zion shall maintain daily records on file as proof of the number of hours worked by each direct service staff providing direct service to members. Each time sheet, equivalent document or data system shall contain the original signature or other independent verification of the member/member's representative after service delivery confirming the hours worked. Zion will provide effective support enhance program activities, using agency vehicles and/or staff personal vehicles. Referrals may come from the Division.

DTT

Zion shall provide specialized sensory-motor, cognitive, communicative, social interaction and behavioral training to promote skill development for some portion of a 24-hour day after school during the year. This program will provide weekday options for school-aged children with developmental disabilities (ages 3-22); teach the consumer based on the ISP outcomes and provide the Support Coordinator the teaching strategies for the outcomes within 10 days of initiating service, tracking data and provide monthly progress report. The Objective and goal are to promote self-esteem, increase social and community safety skills, initiate development of new daily living skills, healthy eating and exercise training, computer training

Zion Compassion Care, LLC (Zion) will actively involve the consumer in developing monthly on-site/community integrated schedule of daily activities schedule. A copy of such schedule will be given to the client and the family, displayed in the facility. Zion will maintain accurate record of the number of hours that each member/client spends daily in the facility by keeping a log of entry and exit at each point daily (Computerize Clock-In System). The program will obtain information and carry out a pre-service orientation within the facility intimating them on how the program intends to meet the ISP outcomes.

Zion shall maintain daily records on file as proof of the number of hours worked by each direct service staff providing direct service to members. Each time sheet, equivalent document or data system. shall contain the original signature or other independent verification of the member/member's representative after service delivery confirming the hours worked. Zion provides effective support enhance program activities, using agency vehicles and/or staff personal vehicles if needed. Zion Shall provide opportunity for the member to develop skills that lead to meaningful days and promote the member's and his/her family's vision of the future.
